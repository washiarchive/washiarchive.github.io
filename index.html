  <h2 style="margin:0 0 6px 0;">Digital Washi Archive</h2>

  <p style="margin:0 0 16px 0; color:#444;">

    Companion page to the Harvard Dataverse collection. DOI-citable datasets of historic Japanese paper (washi) with macro images & standardized metadata.

  </p>


  <div style="display:flex; flex-wrap:wrap; gap:10px; margin-bottom:18px;">

    <div style="background:#fff; border:1px solid #eee; border-radius:12px; padding:14px;">

      <h3 style="margin-top:0; font-size:1.05rem;">Why this matters</h3>

      <p style="margin:0;">

        To my knowledge, this is the <strong>only dedicated washi repository on Harvard Dataverse (as of Oct 16, 2025)</strong>, enabling citable reuse by conservation, design, and DH communities.

      </p>

    </div>


    <div style="background:#fff; border:1px solid #eee; border-radius:12px; padding:14px;">

      <h3 style="margin-top:0; font-size:1.05rem;">Methods</h3>

      <p style="margin:0;">

        Macro imaging pipeline (lens & lighting), standardized metadata (fiber, region, maker/period, usage), provenance & rights notes. See the “Start Here” dataset for details.

      </p>

    </div>


    <div style="background:#fff; border:1px solid #eee; border-radius:12px; padding:14px;">

      <h3 style="margin-top:0; font-size:1.05rem;">Keywords</h3>

      <p style="margin:0;">

        Washi; Japanese paper; Kozo; Gampi; Mitsumata; Handmade paper; Paper conservation; Bast fibers; Fiber analysis; Macro photography.

      </p>

    </div>
